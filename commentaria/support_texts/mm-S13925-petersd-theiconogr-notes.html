24 Martha’s dragon is also described as a descendant of the bonasus, a type of bull or bison: see Pliny, Natural History 8.15.40 and Aristotle, Historia Animalia 9.630a.45. It is possible that the reference to this animal was made as an allusion to Martha’s victory over not only the Judæo–Christian Satan—symbolised by Leviathan—but also over pagan beliefs which had not been totally eradicated in the later Middle Ages. For example, remnants of religions such as Mithraism, which had been widespread in the region of southern Gaul in earlier centuries and whose sacred animal was the bull, may have survived up until the time when the Martha legends were recorded.
