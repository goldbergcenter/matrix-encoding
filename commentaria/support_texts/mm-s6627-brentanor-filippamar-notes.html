1 I should like to thank the mothers and sisters of the Monastero di Santa Filippa Mareri in Borgo San Pietro, and particularly Abbess Sister Margherita Pascalizi, and Sisters Clotilda and Gemma, for their repeated kindnesses to me, including their granting me access to the documents in the convent archives. The most complete and important work on Filippa which includesan edition of the most important documents concerning her is Aniceto Chiappini, "Santa Filippa Mareri a il suo monastero di Borgo S. Pietro de Molito nel Cicolano," Miscellanea Francescana XXII (1921) 65-119; Chiappini's notes (particularly page 65 note 1) include a helpful bibliography. Filippa was noted in the Bulletin of Analecta Bollandiana XII (1893) 480-481; she is included in Benedetto Mazzara, Leggendario Francescano (Venice 1721) II: Vite de Febrajo, 264-266. The saint is praised in a relatively recent and popular work by Luigi Ziliani, La Baronessa Santa, vita e miracoli di S. Filippa Mareri (Bergamo 1935). The most helpful introduction to the Cicolano is still found, I believe, in Domenico Lugini, Memorie storiche della regione Equicola (Rieti 1907). 1 should like to thank my friend Alberto Sestili for introducing me to the area. I intend to write of Filippa and the Mareri in context and at greater length in a forthcoming book on the Church and religion in the city of diocese of Rieti in the thirteenth and fourteenth centuries.
